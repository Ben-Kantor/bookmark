Besides the resources I have made available within this vault, there are a number of useful, freely available D&D 4e resources which I have listed below.

### [Searchable Compendium](https://iws.mx/dnd/?list.name.race)

By far the best resources for viewing character options, monsters, items and more. Essential to building a character.

### [Automated Character Sheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1zIbUhUFqkJkCBLZitgdET7gUaKUOJA5W/view) ([Copy](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1OEqEEYt6Z9XsDPx9n8N5A0zAVK2A-MqWAzLNH5BgiP0/copy))

A google sheet (can also be downloaded for use with libreoffice) which automates most of the math. Recommended for both new and experienced players.

### [Shado's Character Sheets](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/18wnGO4mkvTTv6qE92CaFWf_GUZ1aEW4N?usp=sharing)

Static, printable PDF character sheets. I prefer them over the original ones aestheticly but tastes may vary.

### [All Sourcebooks](./Source)

Fortunately, most official 4e Sourcebooks are available for free online. Some of them are unfortunately scans, but some such as the Dungeon Master's Guide are available with proper tables of contents and copyable text. 

Also available on: [Archive.org](https://archive.org/details/Dnd4e-allbooks/D%26D%204th%20Edition%20-%20Adventurer_s%20Vault%202/)
